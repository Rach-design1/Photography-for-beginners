Taking a Photo

Things to consider before taking a photo: composition, rule of thirds, leading lines, perspective, depth, make your subject pop out.

Take a Photo - Step by step guide

  1. Take off lens cap
  2. Put on lens
  3. Look through viewfinder window
  4. Check the metering bar - you want to be in the middle ideally
  5. Adjust settings as necessary (see exposure triangle)
  6. Take a test shot
  7. If needed alter settings until correctly exposed
  8. Take another test shot
  9. If happy start your shoot
  10. Tip... Have fun and keep checking your shots, as light will change more often if outside and in a studio your position and the models to the lights can also affect the lighting back of camera!

Exposure Triangle

The Exposure Triangle is an Analogy used to explain the main elements that will affect how a Photograph is exposed. ISO, Aperature and Shutter Speed are the three elements that need to be in balance to attain a correctly exposed Photograph.

Practice, Practice, Practice

All Photograpy takes time to learn and practice and having fun is key. Joining a local camera club and booking onto Model Workshops where tuition is provided is a fun and safe environment to learn Photography and make new friends with the same interest!
